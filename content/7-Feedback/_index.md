> In this section, I would like to share a few personal reflections after taking part in the First Cloud AI Journey program, hoping to offer an honest perspective that the FCAJ team can use to keep improving the program for future cohorts.

### Overall Evaluation

**1. Working Environment**  
The office is clean, modern, and quite nice, giving a professional impression from the very first visit. The one thing I found a little unfortunate is that the office is not very large, while the number of participants in the program is fairly high, so I did not always get the chance to come to the office in person to work and practice.

**2. Support from Mentor / Team Admin**  
The mentor was very enthusiastic and always ready to help whenever I ran into difficulties, with fairly quick responses. What I liked was that the mentor usually encouraged me to look into things myself first before giving further help, which let me understand the problem more deeply instead of just receiving a ready-made answer.

**3. Relevance of Work to Academic Major**  
As an Information Technology student, the content on AWS, networking, databases, and application deployment stayed fairly close to my major. The program also expanded into many practical areas I had not had the chance to work on at university, helping me both reinforce the fundamentals and learn new things.

**4. Learning & Skill Development Opportunities**  
In my view, the workload was just right and increased gradually week by week, a good fit for learning and practicing at the same time without being overwhelmed from the start. Through the workshops and hands-on labs, I got familiar with many AWS services while also practicing Hugo, Linux, Docker, and Git/GitHub while building the report website and deploying applications.

**5. Company Culture & Team Spirit**  
What I liked most about the program was the events and the community. They were occasions for me to connect with many people who share a passion for Cloud and AI, to learn from one another, and to feel like part of an energetic community.

**6. Conditions for Interns**  
One thing I was quite happy about is that the office was always cool, with air conditioning throughout working hours, which made it comfortable to attend workshops and sit down to practice. Getting to regularly join the workshops and events was also a valuable learning condition for me.


### Additional Questions

**What I found most satisfying during the internship:**  
If I had to pick one thing, I would choose the always-cool workspace with proper air conditioning — it sounds small, but it helped me focus and stay comfortable during each workshop and practice session. Along with that, the community atmosphere and the events always left a good impression on me.

**What I think the program could improve for future interns:**  
Honestly, I don't see anything really worth complaining about. If I were to give a constructive suggestion, I would only hope the office could arrange for more participants to have the chance to work on-site, since the number of participants is currently quite high relative to the office space.

**If recommending to a friend:**  
I think this really depends on each person's needs and direction. For those who are genuinely interested in Cloud and want a well-structured hands-on environment with an energetic community, I would gladly recommend it. But if someone is heading in a different direction, I would advise them to consider carefully whether it fits, rather than giving an absolute yes.


### Suggestions & Expectations

I have a few small, constructive suggestions:

* Organize more in-depth workshops for those who want to go beyond the basics.
* Add more networking sessions so people can connect and learn from one another.
* Add more practical projects to apply knowledge closer to real work.
* Maintain the community and its connecting activities even after the program ends.

**Would I like to continue this program in the future?**  
Yes. After the internship period, I feel I learned many practical things and made clear progress compared to when I started, so if there is a chance to continue or to join the community's future activities, I would be very willing.
